Technical Investigation

2016-02-06

September 2015 - January 2016

A series of prototypes were developed to investigate the possibilities of developing novel interfaces for digital collaborative work. The prototypes ranged from being low end paper prototypes to technically functional versions.


Multiple prototypes were built to understand the spatial grammar for user interactions. The prototypes asked the participants to complete a certain task like picking up a virtual item and moving it or writing on a surface through physical gestures.
Observations were made in parallel to understand the user behavior with the system within a given space.

Above: Screencapture of indoor positioning data of the mobile device. The system binds the orientation data from the mobile phone and skeleton tracking from a Microsoft Kinect Sensor.

Prototype User Test

Interaction Problem

After testing out the prototypes which used Kinect and phone for indoor positioning, it was observed that the users had trouble establishing hand and eye coordination while putting content on the screens.

Solution

The following prototype shows a novel way of how phone positioning works on a large screen. The back facing camera on the phone communicates continuously with the computer, therefore, generating a color pattern on the screen.

The phone relays the color of the section and the computer recreates a grid within that section. Through several iterations of this process the system can locate the position of the phone on the screen within an accuracy of 4 pixels. This is similar to how NES Zapper works for Duck Hunt Game (1984). Read more about here.
