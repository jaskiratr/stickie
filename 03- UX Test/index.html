UX Test

2016-02-15

Tested the newer version of the prototype with 19 Users. The goal was :

  1. To understand where users would envision such interactions being used.
  2. Ease of grasping the interactions

Prototype Description:

The prototype can be used by a single user to put notes on a single display surface. The server is setup in Cloud9 workspace. The mobile app developed for Android Marshmallow Phones allow users to write and add images onto a drawing canvas. The phone needs to be held against the display surface to put the note on it. The display generates a series of color bars for the system to locate the positon of phone on the screen.

Test Brief:

Youâ€™ve been provided a phone and a display screen. Think of them analogous to a wall and sticky notes. Your activities on it can be shared digitally. The app allows you to write and add images. Place the phone on the screen to post the note from the phone to the display

Observations:

  • The users were fairly comfortable with the learning curve when verbally guided about the way of interacting with the screen.
  • The users held the phone against the screen from the point of pressing the post button to the point when the note appeared on the screen. This time is usually around 2-3 seconds.
  • It was comfortable for the users to intuitively use drawing tools for actions like - clear the canvas, change pen colors and use the eraser tool.

Hypotheses:

  • Stickie is best suited for office environment collaboration. Validated
  • The interactions of Stickie will retain the physical experience of sticky notes. Validated
  • Digitizing information will encourage people to revisit ideas and share it. Validated
  • Inserting images will save significant time, compared to printing and pinning. Validated
  • Accessing to digital media will improve the quality of content generated.
  • Infinite space of digital canvas influences creative judgement.
  • Multiple displays will enhance creative flow and management. Only for advanced users

User Feedback

  • Do a realistic survey of spaces people actually use and the method as well.
  • Marking notes as important needs more work. Add a trash can for discarded notes
  • Remove white space to make it a collaging application.
  • Rainbow is initially confusing. Need better hints. Make it more relevant to posting
  • Silent Brainstorming : Write, collect, post one by one.
  • Not sure about images. Swipe feels weird.
  • Move notes around. Zoom in/out
  • Multiple displays might be confusing for new users.
  • Being able to classify things and query.
  • Show example of content improvement.

Result:

The scope can be expanded to personal homes and public spaces where people post on various surfaces
